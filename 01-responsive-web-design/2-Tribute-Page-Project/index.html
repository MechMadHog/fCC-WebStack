Rodney Mullen

The Godfather of Street

Rodney Mullen revolutionized street skating with a mix of technical brilliance and personal authenticity. He created tricks, companies, and culture with humility, intensity, and focus. This is a tribute to the skater who shaped what modern skateboarding became.

Timeline

1966

Born in Gainesville, Florida

1977

Gets first skateboard
On the condition he always wears pads and quits if injured.

1977

Enters first contest and gets sponsored
Kona Skatepark Boys Freestyle Contest (3rd place) → sponsored by Bruce Walker.

1980

Joins Powell-Peralta’s Bones Brigade
Turns pro after winning Oasis Pro comp in San Diego.

1982

Appears in Powell Peralta's 1982 film

1987

Co-founds World Industries
with Steve Rocco.

2000

Patents new skateboard truck design
Solves hanger-jiggle and wheel bite issues.

2002

Wins Reader’s Choice at TransWorld Awards

2003

Launches Almost Skateboards

2005

Publishes The Mutt
His autobiography: How to Skateboard and Not Kill Yourself

2005

Patents carbon-fiber deck design
With longitudinal beam and inner flanges.

2013

Inducted into the Skateboarding Hall of Fame
