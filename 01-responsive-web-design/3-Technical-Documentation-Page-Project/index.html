Introduction

This is a practical strength notebook focused on a Yolked / Bear‑Mode look: thick back and neck, big shoulders and triceps, upper chest and forearms, strong core and glutes.

Training tools are simple: sandbag, pull‑up bar, dip station, spin‑bike, ab wheel, and bodyweight work. Style is slow, controlled reps with time under tension and weighted stretching.

Nutrition follows a Thermo approach.

tempo example: 3-1-3 (eccentric-pause-concentric)
Muscles

Primary emphasis: Back, Neck, Shoulder, Upper Chest, Core, Glutes, Tricep (long head), and Forearms.

The goal is dense muscle with solid posture and carry—function first, size follows.

  • Back: rows, pull‑ups, sandbag shouldering
  • Neck: controlled neck flexion/extension (band or bodyweight)
  • Shoulder: overhead press, sandbag cleans
  • Upper Chest: high‑angle presses, dips with lean
  • Core: anti‑extension/rotation, ab‑wheel
  • Glutes: hinges, sandbag deadlifts, step‑ups
  • Tricep (long head): overhead extensions, deep dips
  • Forearms: towel hangs, loaded carries
mind-muscle: squeeze hardest at peak contraction ~1s
Equipment

Everything here can be done at home or outside. No fancy machines required.

Use progressive loading, good form, and smart tempo to extract more from less.

  • Sandbag (adjustable fill)
  • Pull‑Up Bar
  • Dip Station (or sturdy parallel bars)
  • Spin‑Bike (cardio / intervals)
  • Ab Wheel
  • Bodyweight calisthenics
sandbag complex: clean → front squat → press → carry (repeat)
Training Focus

Three pillars: weighted stretching, time under tension, and mobility/flexibility. Keep joints happy, move well, then load it.

For sets, control the eccentric, pause when it counts, and finish clean—no ego reps.

  • Weighted stretching at end‑range (safe, controlled)
  • Time Under Tension (30–60s per set target on key lifts)
  • Mobility blocks between sets (hips, t‑spine, lats)
TUT target: 40–50s per set on compounds stretch hold: 30–60s post‑set (lightly loaded)
Programming and Recovery

Simple split, steady progress. Leave 1–2 reps in the tank most days, push occasionally.

Prioritize sleep and walking. Spin‑bike intervals on non‑lifting days or after lifts.

  • 3–4 lifting days per week
  • 2–3 conditioning days (spin‑bike intervals / carries)
  • Daily mobility (10–20 min)
week split: Push / Pull / Legs(+Carry) / Optional Upper intervals: 6x(45s hard / 75s easy) on spin‑bike
Nutrition

Same as PSMF, but now including potatoes (“spuds”), rice, and rice noodles as carb options around training.

Protein, whole foods, hydration, and electrolytes. Egg‑free by necessity.

  • Proteins: beef, chicken, turkey, fish, cottage cheese
  • Carbs: potatoes, rice, rice noodles (timed near workouts)
  • Fats: olive oil, nuts, fatty fish
  • Veg & fruit: pick a mix you actually enjoy
  • Hydration: water + electrolytes, especially on bike / heat days
carb timing: pre 30–90 min / post 0–2h (as needed) protein anchor: ~30–50g per meal
